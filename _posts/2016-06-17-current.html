--- layout: post title: Phew ! Another Year Over ! description: Challenges of writing a blog for a second year. ---

The second year of writing my blog has been like the second day of a five day cycling trip. Very hard going. The first day of cycling is full of excitement and legs are pumped with adrenaline; mile after mile breezes past. But on the second day each turn of the pedals aches and the climbing up a hill sucks the last drop of air out of my lungs. I won’t describe the weather! Similarly with my blog. After the first year of writing my blog, which was great fun, this year I hit the ‘wall’. My ideas for topics dried up, and when I managed to get something down on a page it felt I was probably going to torture the reader. I will always battle with grammar but over the last year it felt that I was loosing. And then there is the pressure of work; family commitments and illness, which added to a head wind, that made each post feel like an impossible turn of the pedal.

Towards the end of this year I stood back from the struggle and tried to do something to revive my enthusiasm for writing and my blog. First I joined Future Learn's free on-line course Introduction to Journalism where I learned about the different styles of journalism which included feature and opinion writing. The new ideas and approaches have refreshed my imagination.

Another area I addressed was grammar. I was unable to answer some test questions for primary school pupils so it was it was time to get back to basics. I bought an excellent book by Sue McMillan, called Easy-Peasy English which is aimed at primary school children, and I have never looked back ( as I type this post John Sutherland’s How Good Is Your Grammar ? popular book is on its way ! )

Leaning for other bloggers is always a good way to pick up on a few tips but I prefer tightly written text. Sadly lots of blogs tend to cover many web pages and by I have paged down several times I have lost interest. However, I started to read editorials and gained a lot of insights about balancing an argument in a few paragraphs, as well as writing in an engaging style. Also, reading a wider range of books has helped. My top book of the year so far is Truman Capote’s In Cold Blood, which details the 1959 murders of four members of the Herbert Clutter family in the small farming community of Holcomb, Kansas. The story is built up through a series of interviews, which makes it feel journalistic and real. Finally, I learned a lot from squeezing the meaning out of a few words in a tweet that tries to reach as wide an audience.

At the end of the second day of cycling, there is a sigh of relief over a few beers. Knowing that the worst is over and muscles know what is ahead and the journey starts to feel achievable. With my blog I feel that a turning point has been reached, and I am looking forward to mixing my imagination with a tighter writing style – it is looking like fun!
