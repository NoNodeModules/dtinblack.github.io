--- layout: post title: Using Pokémon Go To Pick Up Ideas description: Describing the ‘process’ behind developing an idea. ---

It always happens in the morning shower. A thought emerges that clings onto another thought. An idea! It is a strange process – at least it is in my head !

Recently the media has been awash with the latest game to hit the streets, literally, Pokémon Go. The smartphone game, is used to hunt down virtual Pokémon creatures in public areas, currently streets in major cities ( I’m sure that they will be everywhere soon ! ). Standing in the shower the thought that started to take hold: What if the object was not virtual but a toe stubbing solid ?

Another thought. I remembered reading a few years ago about an Oxfam charity shop in Manchester where the story of an object: teddy bear, book, ornament, had been recorded and using their app Shelflife the purchaser could listen to the history of the object. What if we take the technology behind Pokémon Go and use it on real objects overlaid with a virtual fiqure ? Mmmm. Problems start crowding in. How could the information about an object be gathered ? The virtual figures be developed ? … Wikipedia ! Use the collaborative approach behind Wikipedia to gather information about a subject but instead apply it to an object. Another problem starts to clamp around the idea ... trying to type text into a smartphone is not easy ( possibly type the information in at home on a desktop ? ). Ah! another memory. I watched an interview with Andrew Ng, Chief Scientist, Baidu, talking about the exciting new developments in voice recognition technology. The technology could be used to input speech about an object - an ‘object editor’. Lets give it a cool name to get the marketing people excited … “talking object”, “lifenet”, “iThing” (definitely avoid ‘e’ anything – it is so last decade ! ). So there we have it – a freshly mined idea. Using a form of virtual technology to animate solid objects.

Spinning the idea in my mind to see where it goes for possible applications. Objects such as statues, stately houses etc are great for tour guides, gardeners and historians, with lots of potential applications for education. Imagine going around a house picking up parts of a story from virtual people. Using the application with the Internet of Things to check on the health of a toaster ? Or providing guidance on how to fix a tricky plumbing problem with a virtual character pointing to the steps to be taken ? Could it replace QR Codes ( those funny square boxes that can sometimes be seen on an object ), or have more adventurous game such as developing Geocaching ( following clues around the countryside ) ? ....

Now for the business question: how much is the idea worth? Checking the spot price for ideas it is somewhere between a chocolate biscuit and a cappuccino. The real work is taking an idea to market ( it took 20 years for Pokémon Go to reach the streets ) therefore many twists and turns ahead.

Ideas can come at any time and place. But when they do - pump them full of imagination and explore ! I wonder what will happen when I switch on the shower tomorrow ?
