In the previous three posts we covered: the problems of trying to find a clear description of Bayes Theorem on the web, the idea behind probabilities, and the important case of conditional probability. We are now going to take the last step towards a clearer description of the Bayes Theorem.

To illustrate the idea behind Bayes Theorem letâ€™s use the example of two rival football clubs: Red FC and Blue United who have played each other for 20 seasons ( a total of 40 games ). The results are that Red FC have won 15 out of the last 40 games therefore the probability of winning the next game is 15 / 40 or 37.5 % ( good betting odds ? ). However, news arrives that the next game will be played at home. Digging further into previous results we find:

Home Away
Red FC Win 12 3
Red FC Draw 3 2
Red FC Lose 7 13

The results show that Red FC has a better performance at home. Bayes Theorem can be used to update the original probability of 37.5% with the additional information about the team's home and away performance. In this case the probability of Red FC winning when playing at home is ( using mathematical symbols where P() means probability ):

P( Red FC win | home ) = P(home | Red FC win ) * P(Red FC win ) / P( home ),

From the information shown in the table :

P( home | Red FC win ) = 12 / 15,

P( Red FC win ) = 15 / 40,

P( home ) = 20 / 40.

Therefore substituting the values into the formula we get:

P( Red FC win | home ) = ( 12 / 15 * 15 / 40 ) / ( 20 / 40 ) = 1 / 2.

The probability of Red FC wining has been updated with the additional information of home and away performance from 37.5 % to 60% (betting odds are improving!). The result from the match between Red FC play Blue United can be used to update the table and the probabilities can be re-calculated ready for the next game's prediction.

The formula used above describes how a Hypothesis, in this case Red FC winning, is updated with additional information, the Evidence, they are playing at home. The formula used above can be generalised to:

P( H | E ) = P( H | E) * P( H )/P( E ),

which is the usual description of Bayes Theorem found on the web.

Bayes Theorem is a way to update our understanding of a situation with new evidence. The theorem is at the heart of technologies such as Machine Learning, Artificial Intelligence, health diagnostics, preventing terrorist attacks and searching for boats lost at sea. With increasing amounts of data available about how we live, the application of this technology continues to grow and will continue to influence the way that we live.
