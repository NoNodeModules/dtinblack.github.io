To gain greater insight into journalism, Notebook successfully completed the on-line course Introduction to Journalism on Friday 6th May 2016.

The course, was developed by Dr Michael Higgins, Senior Lecture in Journalism at the University of Strathclyde, and a team of internationally-renowned scholars and journalism practitioners and gives you a behind-the-scenes look at the professional world of Journalism. More specifically, Dr Higgins describes what to expect from the course, “you are taught how to recognise and research news stories, to write news features intelligently, to examine how politics impacts on the journalistic process and will find out how investigative journalism can be allowed for more robust reporting”.

The course lasts six weeks and is delivered free by the Future Learn's Massive Online Open Access ( MOOC ) platform. The teaching material is delivered through video based lectures and interviews, supported by text and examples of the different types of news articles. Learning is reinforced by written assignments which are assessed by fellow students, and multiple choice quizzes.

Notebook’s impression of the course was “I had been frustrated by what I felt was poor journalism, the media, in particular social media, seemed to be full of celebreties and personalities rather than hard facts and insightful reporting. When I saw this course I was curious to find out more. It has helped me to sort out the good from bad journalism. I have a better understanding of the relationship between journalists and politicians. But now appreciate the courage, determination and skills of investigative journalism. An unexpected aspect from the course was being introduced to different writing styles and formats which should eventually improve my blog”

The growth in MOOCs doubled between 2014 and 2015 reaching 35 Million students worldwide and is set to continue growing at a rate of nearly 50% annualy for the next few years. They continue to increase access to education to people all around the world. For Notebook the experience, “gave flexible access to insights into journalism, which has changed my attitude to journalists and their work, and I enjoyed every minute !”

The News article above is following the Inverted Pyramid structure which was introduced during the course.
